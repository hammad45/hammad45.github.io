About

I completed my Ph.D. in Computer Science at the University of Oregon in 2025, where I was co-advised by Dr. Hank Childs and Dr. Allen D. Malony. Prior to that, I earned my B.S. in Computer Science from National University of Computer and Emerging Sciences (FAST-NU) in 2018.

Research

My current research at Meta focuses on optimizing large-scale model training and AI infrastructure, with an emphasis on improving efficiency, scalability, and resource utilization in distributed training pipelines.

Prior to joining Meta, my doctoral research centered on high-performance computing (HPC) and parallel I/O optimization. I developed runtime frameworks that analyze, predict, and mitigate I/O bottlenecks in large-scale scientific applications, achieving significant performance improvements through adaptive optimization techniques. This work involved cross-layer performance analysis, runtime prediction, and system-aware optimization, and was conducted in close collaboration with U.S. national laboratories including Lawrence Livermore, Lawrence Berkeley, and Fermilab.

My broader research interests lie at the intersection of AI systems, distributed training optimization, and performance-aware system design, with the goal of building scalable, efficient infrastructure for next-generation AI and data-intensive workloads.
